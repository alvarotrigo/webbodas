## ❗ Biggest conversion leaks (in order)

### 1) Hero promise is too generic

**“Build stunning websites faster than ever”**  
= what every builder says.

Your audience’s real pain:
- “AI gave me something but I can’t iterate cleanly”
- “Webflow is overkill + lock-in”
- “I want something I can export and own”

You should lead with:
- Ownership  
- Visual building  
- Speed via blocks  
- No lock-in (anti-Webflow)

**Better directions:**
- *Build landing pages visually. Export clean code. No lock-in.*
- *The anti-Webflow landing page builder: fast, simple, exportable.*
- *Stop prompt-roulette. Assemble a real landing page in minutes.*

---

### 2) Selling features before outcomes

You list:
- 200+ blocks  
- 20+ skins  
- Responsive  
- Export  

But not the RESULT.

Add outcome bullets under hero:
- Launch a landing page today (not next week)  
- Export HTML/React and host anywhere  
- Easy handoff to a developer later  

---

### 3) No proof section

You need **examples**, not testimonials first.

Add:
## “Built with fullPage Studio”
- 6–12 real site thumbnails  
- Filters: SaaS / Portfolio / App / Event  
- “View live”  
- “Exported HTML” badge  

This is your fastest trust builder.

---

### 4) CTA mismatch

After “Start free,” users think:
- Do I get a URL?
- How do I publish?
- What can I export on free?
- Is this a trap?

Fix with microcopy:
- *Start free — export HTML anytime (Pro unlocks full library + export).*
- Add secondary CTA: **See examples**

---

### 5) Not addressing AI objection

Add a short section:

## AI generates. Studio ships.
- AI = draft  
- Studio = iteration, consistency, exportable code  

One-liner:
> Less prompting. More shipping.

# 💰 Pricing Strategy

---

## Short answer

👉 **Yes, add pricing on the landing page.**

You don’t have a pricing problem.  
You have a **qualification + trust problem.**

---

## ❗ Core mistake in current logic

Current funnel:
Hide price → get email → show value → paywall

Works for:
- Notion  
- Figma  
- Slack  

But your product:
- Low switching cost  
- Many alternatives  
- Low emotional attachment early  

So you capture:
- Curious users  
- Students  
- Non-buyers  
- AI tourists  

👉 Big list ≠ valuable list.

---

## 🧠 What happens when price is hidden

Users assume:
- Probably expensive  
- Might be a trap  
- I’ll check later  
- Not investing time yet  

You lose:
- High-intent buyers  
- Busy founders  
- Comparers  

👉 Your best customers self-select out.

---

## 🎯 Key insight

Showing price:
- Doesn’t kill signups  
- Improves buyer quality  
- Builds trust  

You want:
> Fewer signups, more buyers.

---

## ✅ The hybrid model that fits you

Show pricing but emphasize free:

> Start free. Upgrade when ready.

Display:
- Free  
- $99/year  
- $289 lifetime  

Benefits:
- Clear expectations  
- No surprise paywalls  
- Higher trust  
- Natural upgrades

---

## 🔍 Feedback on your pricing modal

### What’s strong
- Lifetime anchor vs $499  
- Pay once, use forever  
- Clear comparison  
- Lifetime highlighted  

### What’s missing

**1) Risk reversal**
- 14-day refund guarantee  

**2) Value framing**
- Less than hiring a dev for 1 hour  

**3) Ownership reminder**
- Export and host anywhere  

These reduce hesitation.

---

## 🚨 Pricing visibility = trust

Hiding price can signal:
- Low confidence  
- Bait-and-switch  
- Early-stage product  

Your audience prefers:
- Transparency  
- Simple deals  
- Indie honesty  

Your vibe:
> Here’s the deal. Fair and simple.
